Sarah Alnegheimish

I am a Ph.D. student at MIT pursing degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS), advised by Dr. Kalyan Veeramachaneni, and a member of the Data to AI Lab at LIDS. My research interest is in the field of ML & NLP. I work on applying deep learning to solve various challenges, particularly within time series data. I am also interested in open-source development and in building systems that make machine learning usable.

Projects

  • ML Frameworks for Time Series
    Building Sintel ecosystem for time series tasks. Sintel abstracts time series functionalities into primitives and pipelines for classification, forecasting, and anomaly detection tasks. [paper] [code]

  • Unsupervised Time Series Anomaly Detection
    Developing Orion, a machine learning library for unsupervised time series anomaly detection. Given time series data, users select from an array of pipelines, including TadGAN and AER, to identify rare patterns. [paper] [code] [blog] [press]

  • Biases in Large Language Models
    Analyzing and evaluating gender-profession bias in language models by curating a dataset of real-world natural prompts. Comparing template– and natural-based prompts that elicit biased output. [paper] [code]

  • Predictive Framework for Healthcare Data
    Developing Cardea, an ML framework built on top of schemas that support electronic health records (EHR), such as FHIR. It provides an easy-to-use library to develop ML models for numerous problems by applying series of sequential processes. [paper] [code] [press]
