Sarah Alnegheimish

I am an SM student at MIT in the Center of Computational Science and Engineering (CCSE), advised by Dr. Kalyan Veeramachaneni, and a member of the Data to AI Lab at LIDS. My research interest is in the field of Applied Machine Learning and Data Science. I work on applying deep learning to solve various challenges, particularly within the context of time series. I am also interested in building systems that democratize the usage of machine learning.

Projects

  • Orion
    Orion is a machine learning library built for unsupervised time series anomaly detection. Given time series data, the framework identifies rare patterns and flag them for expert review. In this project, we aim to provide a single place where users can find an array of anomaly detection pipelines, provide a systematic approach to evaluate a collection of pipelines, and provide users an easy and seamless experience. [paper] [code] [blog]

  • Cardea
    Cardea is a machine learning library built on top of schemas that support electronic health records (EHR). Our goal is to provide an easy to use library to develop machine learning models from electronic health records by applying series of sequential processes. These processes are abstracted into: data assembling, data labeling, featurizing, and modeling. [paper] [code]

  • Job Space
    There are many determinants to what constitutes an occupation, in this project we aim to analyze this further. We introduce a framework for the construction of a job network that is successful in capturing available job-job transitions. The network is constructed based on similarities between jobs through their skills, and then investigated in terms of educational requirements. We compare educational levels and fields in terms of their susceptibility to automation.
